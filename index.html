About the Legend

Born on the
Nürburgring.

The Porsche 911 GT3 RS represents the absolute pinnacle of what a naturally aspirated, rear-wheel-drive sports car can be. Every element — from its swan-neck rear wing to its track-tuned suspension — exists for one purpose: to make you faster.

With 525 horsepower drawn from a 4.0-liter flat-six engine screaming to 9,000 RPM, and aerodynamics that generate more downforce than any road car in Porsche's history, the GT3 RS isn't just a car. It's a race car with license plates.

Common Questions

FAQ

Everything you need to know.

The GT3 RS is the most extreme road car in Porsche's lineup. While the GT3 focuses on driving purity, the RS adds a motorsport-derived active aerodynamics package — including the swan-neck rear wing and front flics — that generates up to 860 kg of downforce. It also features wider fenders, magnesium center-lock wheels, a more aggressive suspension tune, and an optional Weissach Package that strips out even more weight using carbon fiber body panels.

Yes — the 911 GT3 RS is fully homologated for street use in all major markets. It includes full leather, air conditioning, a sound system, PCM infotainment, and all required safety systems. Porsche's philosophy has always been to build race cars that can legally be driven to the track, raced all day, and driven home.

The 992-generation 911 GT3 RS set a production car lap time of 6 minutes 49.328 seconds on the Nürburgring Nordschleife — making it one of the fastest street-legal cars ever recorded on the 'Green Hell'. This was achieved on Michelin Pilot Sport Cup 2R tires with the standard Weissach Package configuration.

The GT3 RS uses a 4.0-liter naturally aspirated flat-six engine derived directly from the 911 RSR and GT3 Cup racing engines. It produces 525 horsepower at 8,500 RPM and revs to a stratospheric 9,000 RPM redline. There are no turbos — just raw, high-revving mechanical power. Titanium connecting rods and a dry-sump lubrication system allow sustained high-G cornering without oil starvation.

Named after Porsche's famed research and development center in Weissach, Germany, the Weissach Package is an optional weight-reduction package that saves approximately 35 kg. It includes a CFRP front lid and roof, magnesium wheels, front and rear anti-roll bars in carbon fiber, and replaces the interior door handles and several trim pieces with carbon components.

The base MSRP for the 992.2 Porsche 911 GT3 RS starts at approximately $239,300 USD. With the Weissach Package, Paint to Sample, and popular options, most delivered examples exceed $280,000–$320,000. Due to limited production numbers, dealer markups have historically pushed transaction prices well above MSRP.

No — unlike the standard GT3, the GT3 RS is exclusively available with Porsche's 7-speed PDK dual-clutch transmission. The PDK was chosen because its shift speed (under 100ms) outperforms any manual transmission at the GT3 RS's performance level. The system includes custom RS-tuned shift mapping for track use, including a dedicated "Sport+" mode with maximum aggressiveness.
